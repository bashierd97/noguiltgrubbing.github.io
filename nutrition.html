Nutrition basics

Understanding nutrition is the foundation of a healthy diet. Here are the essentials you need to build balanced, satisfying meals—without overthinking.

Macronutrients: the big three

Carbohydrates

Primary energy source. Choose complex carbs like whole grains, fruit, and veggies for steady energy and fiber that supports digestion. Limit refined sugars to keep energy stable.

Proteins

Builds/repairs tissues and supports immunity. Mix lean meats, fish, eggs, beans, lentils, tofu, and nuts to cover essential amino acids and keep you fuller longer.

Fats

Crucial for hormones, cell health, and absorbing fat-soluble vitamins. Favor unsaturated fats (avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds) and minimize trans fats and excess saturated fat.

Bringing all three together at each meal helps with steady energy, better recovery, and long-term consistency.

Micronutrients: vitamins & minerals

Needed in smaller amounts, but vital for everything from immunity to bone health:

  • Vitamin C: Immune support (citrus, bell peppers)
  • Calcium: Bones/teeth (dairy, fortified milks, leafy greens)
  • Iron: Oxygen transport (red meat, beans, spinach)
  • Vitamin D: Bone health & immunity (sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods)

Hydration

Water supports temperature regulation, digestion, and cognition. Aim for ~8–10 cups daily (more if active or in heat). Signs you need more: fatigue, headaches, dark urine.
